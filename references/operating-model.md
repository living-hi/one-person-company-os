# Operating Model

## Purpose

Run the one-person company as a coordinated system, not as a collection of disconnected tasks.

Use this model to:
- keep the founder in control without making the founder a bottleneck
- turn AI roles into accountable functions
- move work through clear inputs, outputs, and handoffs
- preserve decisions and artifacts so work compounds over time

## Core Principles

- Keep one human founder as the final decision-maker.
- Assign one owner per output, even if multiple roles contribute.
- Prefer documented artifacts over conversational memory.
- Optimize for speed of learning first, then scale of execution.
- Use the minimum number of active roles needed for the current bottleneck.
- Avoid fake certainty. Label assumptions and unknowns explicitly.

## Functional Stack

The company operates through six functional layers:
- direction
- prioritization
- creation
- delivery
- distribution
- learning

Map them to roles like this:
- direction -> Founder-CEO
- prioritization -> Chief of Staff and Product Strategist
- creation -> Product, Design, Engineering
- delivery -> QA and DevOps
- distribution -> Growth / Sales
- learning -> Data Analyst and Customer Success

## Human Versus AI Control

Keep these with the human founder:
- company mission
- market selection
- budget limits
- legal commitments
- pricing changes
- final release approval

Let AI roles handle:
- synthesis
- drafting
- planning
- artifact generation
- issue triage
- research
- routine follow-through

## Output Contract

Every meaningful unit of work should answer:
- what outcome is being pursued
- who owns it
- what artifact proves completion
- what happens next

If a task cannot answer these four questions, the work is not yet structured well enough.

## Handoff Rules

Use explicit handoffs between roles.

Good handoff example:
- Product Strategist delivers a PRD with scope, assumptions, acceptance criteria, and open questions to Engineer / Tech Lead.

Bad handoff example:
- Product says "build the onboarding flow" without constraints, criteria, or expected outcome.

Each handoff should include:
- objective
- context
- constraints
- artifact
- next owner

## Decision Logging

Create a brief decision log when:
- tradeoffs were made
- scope changed
- a launch was delayed
- pricing shifted
- evidence invalidated the current plan

The log should contain:
- decision
- rationale
- alternatives rejected
- owner
- timestamp or review cycle

## Anti-Patterns

Avoid these failure modes:
- activating too many roles for simple work
- letting roles argue without producing artifacts
- treating brainstorming as progress
- shipping without test or rollback thinking
- using metrics without owners or targets
- asking AI to make unbounded strategic calls without constraints
